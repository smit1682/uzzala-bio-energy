From Waste to Energy

Four stages turn organic waste into pipeline-grade renewable fuel. Scroll to follow the energy.

00
6+ feedstock types
Stage 01 — Intake

Waste Collection

Organic feedstock — pressmud, sugarcane bagasse, cow dung, poultry waste, kitchen waste, and municipal solid waste — is collected and transported to the plant.

00
32–38°C mesophilic
Stage 02 — Conversion

Anaerobic Digestion

Feedstock is processed in sealed digester tanks where microorganisms break down organic matter in the absence of oxygen, producing raw biogas rich in methane.

00
97%+ methane purity
Stage 03 — Refinement

Gas Purification

Raw biogas is purified through H₂S removal, water scrubbing, PSA, and membrane separation to achieve 97%+ methane purity — IS 16087:2016 compliant.

00
IS 16087:2016
Stage 04 — Delivery

CBG Distribution

Compressed Biogas is stored in high-pressure cylinders and distributed to fueling stations, industrial consumers, and the gas grid — clean, renewable, carbon-negative fuel.

Organic Fertilizer

The sludge from biogas production can be used as organic fertilizer which functions as plant growth stimulator and bio-fungicide. Biogas residue fertilizer is not only with high quality, but also a good soil conditioner.

According to the determination, applying biogas organic fertilizer can increase soil 0.17-0.6% of organic matter content, enhance 0.003-0.005% of total nitrogen and improve 0.01-0.03% of total phosphorus.

Using sludge from biogas production as a high-quality organic soil enhancement. Natural, sustainable, and chemical-free.

Improves soil structure, increases water retention, enhances nutrient availability, and promotes microbial activity in the soil ecosystem.

Reduces dependency on chemical fertilizers, lowers agricultural costs, closes the waste-to-value loop, and creates an additional revenue stream.
